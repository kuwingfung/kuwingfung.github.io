Project for the course, Computer Game Development, at PolyU. We developed an innovative game which inspired by collective primary school memories of Hong Kong teentagers playing paper-folded aircraft back in their childhood. Paper Warfare is a top-down combat game for the PC that developed in Unity. Players customize their own self-made paper aircraft, control them on the battlefield to duel with other aircraft creations from their opponents. In this work, we present 2 hours of quality gameplay content including story mode and versus mode. This game has been selected as 'Best Project Work' by the accessors. We are currently planning to put the game on Steam platform for commerical purpose.

Paper Warfare is a top-down combat game for the PC that was developed with Unity. It is a proof of concept game that originated from a childhood game “Paper Aircraft Flick and Hit” game in Hong Kong primary schools, where teenagers played paper-folded aircraft back in their childhood. In that real-life game, each player first uses a piece of paper to fold their own aircraft, then they put their self-made aircraft onto the desk, then flick their aircraft and try their best to knock out their opponent. The first aircraft that gets knocked out of the desk will lose the game. Our game is mainly based on this mini-game concept, as we wish to arouse people with those nostalgic memories. 

In the game, players can customize their paper aircraft, and control them on the battlefield to duel with other aircraft creations by their opponents. We employ this in real-time control to make the game more intense and added different elements to try to make the game fun.
